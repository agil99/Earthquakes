Background Information

  • The average number of earthquakes with a magnitude of 3 or higher in a year was only 21. that has greatly increased to 600+ in 2014 and 1000+ in 2015.
  • In response to the sudden and dramatic increase in seismic activity, the United States Geological Survey(USGS) has deployed seismic stations in order to better understand the earthquakes.
  • The USGS uses numerical simulations of earth processes to evaluate the relationship between fluid injection and earthquakes, and to predict the effects of new and current injection projects.
  • The USGS has the ability to provide an earthquake forecast
